On the Internet, Nobody Knows You're A Dog

     

The year is 1998.

The Information Superhighway is still a vast unexplored space of possibilities. Back in those days, you could be anything you wanted to be on the Internet. You couldn't even be sure whether the 'netizen' on the other end of your chat line was even human.

On The Internet, Nobody Knows You're A Dog is a love letter to Web 1.0 culture in the form of a rhythm game. You play as a dog chatting in an AOL Instant Messenger chat room. Will you blend in, or will you slip up and let everyone realize you're actually a dog?

Controller and gameplay

On The Internet, Nobody Knows You're A Dog is a rhythm game in the vein of Guitar Hero or Dance Dance Revolution. To play, you "type" on a keyboard using a pair of dog paw controllers, drumming along to MIDI renditions of the greatest hits of the late 90s and early 2000s. Play accurately enough, and you'll trick the other chatters into thinking you're human; hit too many wrong keys, and they'll suspect something is up!

The game's hardware consists of a sturdy mechanical keyboard and custom dog paw controllers.

Concepts for Final Design

By the time GDC rolls around, the game will feature:

  • Multiple visual "stories" to play during songs, with funny chat logs and failure states to make the game entertaining for spectators.
  • Multiple difficulty modes that subdivide the keyboard into more "buttons" for more skillful players.
  • A larger selection of songs and stepcharts. We're hoping to work with our indie game dev friends to include MIDI versions of songs from well-known indie games!
